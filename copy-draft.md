# Global Observations & Recommendations

## What's Working in the Current Copy

1. **"We run your hotel like it's ours."** -- The strongest line on the site. Keep it. Use it more.
2. **The overall tone is warm and professional** -- it doesn't feel corporate or buzzwordy. Good instincts.
3. **The four-step "How We Work" section** -- the structure is right, just needs sharper copy.
4. **The about teaser on the homepage** -- leading with Chris's personal accessibility ("Your call goes to our leadership team, not a call center") is the right instinct.

## What Needs Fixing

1. **The hero headline is forgettable.** "Your Hotel. Expertly Managed." could be any management company. It needs to create tension or speak to a specific desire.
2. **Pain points are too polished.** The problem section describes pain abstractly ("OTA channel chaos") instead of showing it ("Your Expedia listing hasn't been updated since last quarter and you're losing bookings to the hotel across the street").
3. **Service descriptions are feature-focused.** "Daily operations oversight and quality standards enforcement" tells you what they do, not why you should care. Every bullet should answer: "so what?"
4. **The trust bar is passive.** "Full-Service Operations" is a description, not a trust signal. Trust signals should make someone think "these people are different" or "these people are credible."
5. **CTAs could be warmer.** "Schedule a Discovery Call" is fine, but "Let's Talk" (already used on the hero) is better. The best CTA on the site is the one that doesn't exist yet: "Just Call Chris."
